Featured Student

Jamie Morrow is a Junior at Washington University. She came to WashU undecided about her major and decided to take a year to figure things out by covering courses in all of the ArtSci requirements. She gravitated towards humanities courses, especially history and creative writing. Finally she discoverd her passion in an art history class taught by Professor William Wallace, an expert on Michaelangelo. She decided to gain a double major in the Sam Fox school and next year she will study abroad in Italy with the Florence-semester program. Jamie isn't sure what she wants to do after graduation but she is building her resume by working as a docent at MFA in Boston this summer.

Submit Advice

  • Some of the prerequisites at WashU are not really necessary. For example, General Chemistry 111A/112A is not at all necessary for Biology 2960/2970.

  • Don't feel like you need to do basic science research as a pre-med. Most people admitted to medical school did not complete any research as undergrads and if anything, the schools prefer clinical research.

  • Check out the ArtSci event calendar once in a while. It's easy to miss but its a great way to learn more about your area of interest and connect with teachers and students.

  • Reach out to students in your classes. There is always someone else looking for someone to work with.

  • Start writing your resume now! Your life will be so much easier when you start applying to internships and jobs.
