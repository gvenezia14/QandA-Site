---------------- St. Louis ------------------

St. Louis has many inexpensive options for an outing. You don't need to be confined to the WashU bubble!

Submit a Review

Bars and Restaurants

Pastaria

Review: Great restaurant with a special night out with friends. The pistachio ravioli is my favorite! ....Kind of a hike by metro but well worth the trip.

Taste Bar

Review: Delicious food and cocktails but this place can really break the bank.

Lee's Chicken

Review: I love to stop by here with my friends after a night out. Not the healthiest option but they really know how to do fried chicken right.

Parks and Scenic routes

Forest Park

Review: Great bike and running routes - you can bike all the way to the medical school through the park. Also forest park contains the art museum, zoo, and history amont other attractions.

The Offsets

Review: Privately owned quarry with steep cliffs around the edges so you can jump in. Great day trip with friends.

National Parks

Review: Missouri and Illinois have a host of beautiful national parks nearby. Click here for a list of the parks closest to WashU

Free Events

Grove Fest

Review:This festival located over in the grove takes place once a year. With food, crafts, and music, it is a great way to spend the afternoon.

KWUR week

Review: Everyone at WashU knows about WILD, but kwur week gets much less publicicty. This is a week of free concerts put on by WashU's own radio station.

Twilight Tuesdays at Missouri History Museum

Review: Free concerts weekly at the history museum. The best one I attended was a Frank Sinatra cover band.

Shakespeare in the Park

Review: Each summer, there is a Shakespeare performance in forest park. Many people like to pack a picnic and come early, scouting out the best spots. Be prepared for rain!

Outings

St. Louis Symphony Orchestra

Review: $10 student tickets are available! St. Louis has one of the country's best symphony orchestras and Powell hall is a beautiful place that everyone should visit.

The Kingsbury Ensemble

Review: Inexpensive classical concerts that occur around St. Louis. I attended "Venice and the Orient" this season and not only was there great music, but wine and cheese were served at intermission!

Bump and Hustle

Review: If you're looking to go to party but your tired of WashU, Bump and Hustle is ideal. Nice ambiance and great music.
